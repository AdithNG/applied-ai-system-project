# Music Mood Reference

## happy
Happy music creates feelings of joy, optimism, and uplift. Musically, it tends to use major keys, bright timbres, upbeat tempos (120+ BPM), and lyrics celebrating positive experiences. The valence — the musical "positivity" score — is typically high. Happy music is socially activating: it makes people want to move, sing along, or share. It suits moments of celebration, exercise, or simply wanting an emotional boost. Examples include summer anthems, feel-good pop, and uptempo funk.

## chill
Chill music induces calm, ease, and comfortable relaxation without putting the listener to sleep. It typically features mid-range tempos (70–110 BPM), smooth textures, and minimal abrasive elements. It may be introspective or social but never demanding. Chill music works as background music for studying, casual socializing, or winding down after activity. Unlike ambient, chill music usually retains a discernible groove or melody. Lo-fi hip-hop, mellow indie pop, and soft electronic music are common chill subgenres.

## intense
Intense music is emotionally charged and demanding — it grabs attention and doesn't let go. It often features high tempo, loud dynamics, driving rhythms, minor keys, or aggressive instrumentation. The emotional register can be anger, urgency, determination, or adrenaline. Intense music suits high-focus work, athletic training, or cathartic emotional release. Heavy rock, aggressive electronic, and cinematic orchestral builds are all capable of intensity. The key quality is that the music commands the listener's full attention.

## sad
Sad music evokes melancholy, longing, or grief. It typically uses minor keys, slower tempos, and lyrical themes of loss or heartbreak. Interestingly, sad music often provides emotional comfort — listeners use it to feel understood or to process difficult emotions. A low valence score is typical. Acoustic instrumentation, restrained production, and expressive vocals amplify sadness. Artists like Elliott Smith, Bon Iver, and Lana Del Rey are associated with deeply melancholic music. Sad music can paradoxically be soothing and cathartic.

## energetic
Energetic music prioritizes physical drive and momentum. High BPM (130+), strong downbeats, compressed dynamics, and bright or aggressive production combine to push the listener into movement or heightened alertness. Energetic music differs from intense in that it need not be emotionally heavy — it can be joyful and high-energy simultaneously. It suits workouts, parties, and any context where the goal is sustained physical or mental activation. EDM drops, sprint-pace hip-hop, and driving rock are all energetic.

## romantic
Romantic music is intimate, warm, and emotionally tender. It creates an atmosphere of closeness, longing, or affection. Tempos are moderate, dynamics are often soft, and instrumentation (piano, strings, gentle guitar) favors warmth over power. Lyrics focus on connection, desire, and vulnerability. Romantic music suits date settings, quiet evenings, or moments of personal reflection on relationships. R&B, soft pop, and certain jazz pieces are naturally romantic. High valence combined with low energy often characterizes this mood.

## melancholic
Melancholic music occupies a specific emotional territory: not purely sad, but reflective and bittersweet. It often combines beautiful melodies with minor harmonies, creating a wistful quality — the feeling of something precious that has passed or is out of reach. Unlike sad music, melancholic music isn't about raw grief but about nuanced emotional complexity. It appeals to listeners who want music that honors the full depth of human experience. Post-rock, cinematic scores, and certain classical pieces often achieve this quality.

## focused
Focused music serves as a cognitive aid — it reduces distraction without demanding attention. Ideal focused music has a steady, non-disruptive rhythm; minimal lyrics; and a consistent sonic texture that occupies the background of consciousness without pulling focus. Tempos are moderate, dynamics are flat (no sudden changes), and emotional content is neutral to slightly positive. Lo-fi hip-hop and certain ambient electronic music are engineered for this purpose. The goal is flow state — absorbed, productive, undistracted work.

## relaxed
Relaxed music promotes physical and mental ease without necessarily inducing sleep or deep introspection. It has lower energy than chill, slower tempo, softer dynamics, and a generally unhurried quality. Unlike ambient music, relaxed music retains recognizable structure and melody. It suits recovery, unwinding, reading, or transitioning from active to restful states. Acoustic guitar, soft piano, and slow jazz are common vehicles for relaxed music. The emotional quality is one of comfort and ease.
